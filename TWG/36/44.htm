This game was obviously somewhat experimental, and I'm still not quite sure whether it had balance issues or skittles just didn't play very well. Early in making this game, I realized that there weren't that many colors of skittles (at least, not traditionally) and I though it would be neat to have a game with lots of spies and the wolves not knowing one another. It's another one of those slightly tricky situations where, had there been one less wolf, I think the game would have been pretty strongly stacked against wolves, but as was, it was possibly slightly in wolf favor. I imagine, though, that the game might have gone rather differently if more people had taken notice of this, in the first post:

Quote from: epplant on January 16, 2013, 01:35:24 PM
Had a fun idea about making it a bit more non-vanilla, so we'll stick it in there to see how it works. I mean disregarding the fact that this is actually a rainbow game anyways. To give you a hint, it relates to how the flavor plays out and avoiding any duplicates/made-up skittles.

It took a little while for people to start becoming suspicious of all the duplicates. Nonetheless, this was a sort of fun game to host because, for the first few days, the game was very, very close to swinging to one side or the other, but never quite made it there. And then it fell quite quickly.

Colors were actually totally randomized, but attached to roles, although there were a few somewhat funny coincidences (including mystic's "Tastes like purple") that I noticed afterwards.

I didn't think that allowing dieter to post the PMs would have quite the effect that it did (especially since it would be different from posting host PMs), but in hindsight that was a poor decision and I probably wouldn't do it again. Also, I knew that the game would have been over for skittles if dieter was lynched instead of dr.agon, but the opposite was not necessarily true. I had considered notifying players that were entered into communication pairs that they did not inherently know their partner's alignment, but I decided against it because I didn't want to influence any wolf plans to trick human players. This seems to have worked out well for Angrynarwhal, but not for Dr.Agon, and I'm not sure whether I would do it differently in a similar situation. Possibly it would be a better idea in the end, though, especially before the connecting mechanism was made clear to disconnected wolves.

There were a few player decisions that confused me a bit (for instance, that Iron never connected himself to Valex, despite knowing his alignment). I'm not sure how things would have been different otherwise.

Possibly the funniest moment in the game for me was when Valex guarded Dr.Agon after he had been lynched.

N3 role changes came about because I realized in retrospect that I basically had not given roles to 3 players. Whoops.

The yellow role came from the role "hider" I encountered on a different forum (where it was actually considered "vanilla"). I think it's a cool role that requires some interesting strategy, especially when played by a human. Potentially very powerful, but with a p big risk.

Anyways, yeah. Hope a few careless things didn't fuck stuff up too much. I might have had other thoughts but I don't remember what they are offhand. Ah well.
