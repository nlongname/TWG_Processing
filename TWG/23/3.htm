That must be what's off about me. I don't have computer problems or tornado damage to recover from so hey why am I not posting more? She's got a good point. I might be a wolf! I better keep an eye on myself, just in case.

In the meantime, though, if Ben's serious about coming out as seer day one and viewing That_Kid, we might want to think this one through a bit.

We've got ten players. It's safe to assume that we've only got one seer as a result. Two wolves seems light, but three seems like it would be a bit excessive, so maybe we've got two wolves and a mark when one dies? I dunno. I always think of marks as being stronger than standard random wolves, but conventional wisdom seems to place them as "half a wolf." If we've only got two wolves, and Ben is the seer, then it's certainly the best move to come right out and declare it. Even if he then dies the following night, wolves are majorly fucked. But if there's only two wolves, they will NOT attempt fakeseering because any cardflip type role would reveal the treachery and wolves would be fucked again. So if there are two wolves, Ben is definitely the seer.

The sheer level by which wolves are fucked in that scenario makes me think there's three wolves. In this case, we must tread much more carefully. In the scenario in which Ben is the seer, wolves will kill him off night one (unless they are reading this and try to WIFOM us) to prevent further damage. That means that day 2 will have eight players and two wolves, with no seer. If we screw up in killing a wolf twice without the seer's guidance, we lose. However, if Ben is not the seer and he's using the wolves' numbers to safely fakeseer us, day 2 will leave us with no room for error.

Both those scenarios presume we kill That_Kid, of course. If we kill Ben and he is not the seer, yay, two wolves left and probably a seer hiding somewhere. That's good numbers. However, if we kill him and he IS the seer, whoops, we are fucked as described above: day 2 comes as 5 vs. 3 and no seer for the humans.

Game theorying this shit up, we have a 2x2 matrix of possibilities: we either kill Ben or That_Kid, and Ben either is or is not the seer. To recap:
A) Ben seer, Ben dead: bad.
B) Ben seer, Kid dead: good.
C) Ben wolf, Ben dead: good.
D) Ben wolf, Kid dead: bad.
Wooooo 50/50. I don't like that. I tend to lose coinflips. So let's see if we can improve them a bit. C) is better for us than B), and A) is worse for us than D). However, that does not mean that C) = the opposite of A). When calculating risk-benefit stuff, you need to consider what is actually more likely to happen, and there's one important aspect in all this that I haven't yet brought up: It's day 1.

A day one fakeseer claim would be stupid as hell. The only person who could validate your claim of accuracy is the one you'd be accusing of being a wolf (and presumably, you don't reveal seerness unless you've found a wolf). You'd have to convince everyone of your claim, and you're starting with a vote against you. From a wolfy tactical standpoint, it's a suicide charge that would only work if no one believed you're dumb enough to try it. Ben seems rational enough to not dare to try it. On the flip side, it was discussed last game that a seer-for-wolf trade is almost always worth it, and logically that would extend to day one. So Ben, as a seer, would have slightly additional reason to reveal himself as soon as he found a wolf. So back to that 2x2 matrix, we can reduce our choices to either believing Ben or not.

tl;dr version:

If Ben is a seer, he is making a good tactical play. If Ben is a wolf, he would be making a stupid gambit that has a huge chance of backfiring and would not necessarily win the game anyways.

By this reasoning, I'm going to believe him and vote That_Kid.
