No, he was doing what he could to stay out of the limelight. It's usually how wolves try to avoid getting lynched. The nice thing about being a seer is that you can actually confirm suspicions yourself.

dr. agon claims to be a chaplain, and he claims that Haplo was a guardian. Now here's the funny thing about that: let's suppose hypothetically that he is a wolf. He is being drawn under fire by a seer. The only way he can save himself is by convincing people that he's got an important role (as a "normal human" defense, while realistically more likely, is less likely to convince people to not lynch him). So he decides to make the best of it, and call out the guardian while he's at it. That's right, I'm saying he's claiming Haplo as the guardian to see if the REAL guardian will stand up and say "no, dr. agon is lying, because I'M the guardian!", and in the doctor's ensuing sacrifice, he will have guaranteed the two biggest human roles to get knocked out. It's imperative that the wolves know who the guardian is NOW, because if there is still a guardian, he will attempt to guard me. If a guardian does the dumb thing and reveals themselves, THAT person will be the one nightkilled tonight.

Funny thing is, though, we don't know that there IS a guardian in this game. So if such a player does NOT reveal themselves, the wolves have two choices: 1) try to NK me, and be prepared to only confirm my humanity should a guardian be in the game, or 2) NK someone else and hope to rally the votes against me to get me lynched (and hopefully hit the real guardian while doing so). Kind of a tricky situation for them, eh?

Now, let's assume the hypothetical situation in which I was a false seer (and did not know it, because I stand by my claim that as far as I know, the goggles show me an aura of truth). I am still playing for the humans. If it would be preferred, I can check someone who we all agree as being pretty human, and I'll see whether the goggles are misleading me or not. Assuming I don't get nightkilled.

But what if I'm a false seer playing for the wolves, and I'm knowingly trying to screw the players? You'll notice I only ever stated that I can tell whether a player is good or evil. Never could I claim to know roles, or even "wolf status" as eep pointed out. Given the flavor of the game, I would say that "evil" means "wolf" here, since the story seems to indicate serial killers more than werewolf attacks. But I'll leave that window of possibility open. Now, wouldn't it be AWFULLY convenient for me to hit a human chaplain role night one with my false-seering? My real reasoning for checking him night one was that he seemed suspicious to me. A chaplain, however, would not have had any special information day one in this game, thus he should not have seemed any more suspicious than anyone else to a false seer. What I'm saying is that as a good seer, I had reason to suspect dr. agon and thus view him. As a false seer, however, I would have had no such reason. This reasoning goes EVEN FURTHER if I was just a normal wolf, in that I COULD NOT HAVE KNOWN if dr. agon was a chaplain, but it'd be MIGHTY CONVENIENT for me to just randomly call him out on day two and potentially get myself lynched.

My point is, the ONLY possible explanation for my card-flip on day two is that I was a good, normal seer who suspected dr. agon and checked him, and had my suspicions confirmed. That's the only reason I'd be giving my role up like this.
