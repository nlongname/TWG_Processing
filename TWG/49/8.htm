strategy? is there anything so false as a strategy in vanilla twg?

Just wondering if there was a reason.

I'm going back to Trient. Swoop is probably just incompetent and not a wolf.

The reason lynching inactives is good is because a wolf is, at base probability, equally likely to be among them.
Historically we've never, that I can think of, seen someone be inactive only because they're a wolf (or someone who is normally inactive pop in more as wolf), so I think that base 25% chance of wolfishness is probably pretty accurate.
They don't contribute things. They're someone we'll never have info on. If they're an aux, they'll at least tell us that much, I would think. If they can't, then they're probably too busy to use their powers usefully.

Now, those are all reasons that it's OK to lynch an inactive. Other plans are definitely superior at times. If an inactive can give a response then it's awesome to have votes on them. If they don't appear, it's probably a neutral lynch or a crapshoot.
But putting a vote on someone with 0 reason at all? That doesn't seem as useful as a vote with a real observation attached.

Vanilla TWG is all about breaking the Catch-22 that you can't vote without information and can't gain information without votes. There are ways to do that, and I look at who is willing to talk first when I'm deciding who is and isn't wolfy.

So, duh, there is indeed a strategy in vanilla TWG, or it wouldn't be a game any of us enjoy.
