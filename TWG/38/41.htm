I think your biggest disadvantage, Tri, is that there is not much else presenting itself for lynch candidacy.

It's really hard to push for an inactive to get lynched, because there are few arguments against them personally.


10 players. 7 are active. Exactly 3 are guards. ~3 are wolves. Eep is the leader.

Lynch a non-eep player at random. You have a ~33% chance of hitting a wolf (assuming we have killed none) and exactly a 33% chance of hitting a guard. This is neat because it means we have exactly a 66% chance of hitting a nonguard. Furthermore, guards can claim (vanillas can't, wolves can but will be punished if it fails to work).

Lynch an inactive player at random (AJ, Reaper, Modi). Those same ratios apply - 66% chance of not hitting a guard. Probably about 33% of hitting a wolf; same for guard. Guard can also speak up for themselves - if they don't, they weren't contributing anything but a number anyway.

The only real risk is if someone like AJ is not reading, is a guard, and gets lynched before claiming. We'll still know he was a guard, but too late to save him. Not too late to use the info.



So in this game, where lying low is rather different than usual, perhaps we have more incentive than normal to hit an inactive (something I'm usually in favor of anyway). That said, lynching someone who you really think is a wolf is obviously way better, so if your gut screams Tri or Phil, that's fine too.
