In this game, wolves NEED to kill guards. It's not a matter of simply trying to stay under cover and reach dominance through numbers. They have to do a great deal of hunting to try to find the guards in a sea of vanillas (and eep) trying to protact their guards. If I HAD to guess why Danny was killed it was because they thought he, out of all players, was likely a guard. There may have been some other reasons (day voting, interaction with others) but these would be secondary to their primary goal of selecting a guard. They can dissect the day any which way, but they CANNOT win until the guards are gone, and they can NK eep. The longer they dawdle, the longer they spend not winning. I've been going through the game with the assumption we are dealing with four wolves, because this game seems very tipped in our favor unless they have considerable numbers.
