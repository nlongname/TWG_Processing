-these are all additional reasons why guards in danger of being lynched should claim. and that we should think very carefully about whether or not to believe them and not just immediately learn towards disbelief just because wolves can do it too

-using activity as a basis for lynching is as productive as random lynching. possibly less since an inactive guard could prove beneficial, as eep pointed out (wolves won't think to or won't want to nk)

another thing i just thought of that is fundamentally different is that we're ALWAYS aware of how close the wolves are to winning. we always know how many of their targets are alive. i think this is another reason missing an opportunity to lynch a wolf is less bad, and a reason it's okay to leave guard claims alive, even if more likely wolf than guard (still/always a chance they're truthful). and, as everyone has pointed out- wolves are likely to guard claim too. so we'll probably have a good idea of how many wolves we've gotten (if a claim isn't revealed as guard, they're probably wolf). we can always keep track of the progress of the game and use that to our advantage. we'll always know how dire it is at the moment to kill them.

additionally and somewhat contradictory, this is a reason for wolves to not claim guardian, so that they're numbers are less well known, and because of that further reason to listen to guard claims, as there is NO downside to guards in danger of being lynched to claim.
