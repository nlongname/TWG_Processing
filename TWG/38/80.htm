i think too many people get excited to write flavor/send out pms and don't realize how silly that will often make the game. i'm reminded of the h2g2 twg on fb that was basically awful in that regard, iirc.

i often spend weeks on writing the flavor, then reading and re-reading them to make sure that the pms i'm sending out won't inadvertently break the game. it's frustrating to see people not put that much effort into it. it's also unfortunate that i don't like to and don't want to run a vanilla game, so the actual effort i put into that part of play doesn't end up being very useful since the mechanics themselves end up being tailored to the wrong audience.

anyway i want encourage writers to really keep in mind what they are doing and not let the excitement of controlling your flavor and setup to ruin your chances of actually running the game fairly. even the most simple of mechanics can and will be broken by lazy pm work or other unclear rules. PLAY THROUGH YOUR GAME a few times with many different scenarios to see what happens. a couple of test runs with semi-random votes would have found the tiebreaker issue in this game extremely quickly.

i mean, it was a good attempt at fixing the "ties" problem (which we don't really have a good way around yet, but this also lets each host put their own mark on the game) but ended up being quite problematic.
