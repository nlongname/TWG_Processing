To put it another way, I'm pissed off because there's this group of people who seem to be trying to find Freddie Krueger when they should be looking for Ted Bundy.

Think for a moment about various metas that have been introduced into the game. Third vote on any bandwagon is likely to be a wolf. Players who congratulate the guardian on a successful protection is likely to be a wolf. People who pick up on tells/hints easily, especially with less experience/skill, are more likely to be mafia. And so on and so forth.

Fundamentally, what is it about these things that makes them possible tells? It certainly isn't something magical about the number 3 that all players become attracted to once they become wolves. It's the fact that wolves do not like to challenge; they prefer to charm. That's what makes matt miller so fucking lethal as a wolf; being likeable for pretty much the entire game comes naturally to him, as well as manipulating people into doing things his way, even against their better judgement.

On the other hand, humans, especially humans with more or less expendable roles, tend to be substantially more ballsy. Humans will declare that something doesn't matter right now, apply votes based on intuition rather than "safe" reasoning, and question what's going on for the purpose of figuring it out, not pushing a goal. Humans do not need to try to pass beneath the eyes of the seer, to protect known teammates, or to draw attention to innocent players in the hopes of falsely incriminating them. Humans simply do not make as much of an effort to play it safe, because they do not need to play it safe.

If any player is really, totally fixated on the idea that I am guilty because of this one sentence:

Quote from: eep on December 13, 2013, 07:08:44 PM
Plus, there's not really any reason to read into wolf kills at this point in the game.

Then I seriously suggest that you examine the implications that you believe come along with it. Did I advocate not looking at the wolf kill at any point in the game? No. I said that, at present, the amount the we could draw from the kill did not strike me as sufficient to justify a focus on it. Furthermore,

Quote from: AngryNarwhal on December 14, 2013, 03:38:41 PM
As far as eep, yeah I'd say your response is also fairly reasonable, but I find it very telling that you want to draw attention away from the one piece of evidence that a wolf can't hide, cover up, distort, lie about, etc.

This is not even true. Any exercise in trying to determine the motivation behind a particular night kill is futile, because a wolf chooses the kill, typically, with some thought regarding the effect it will have. Putting aside possibilities like bombs, vigilantes, and other means by which a wolf kill might be obfuscated, a wolf can deliberately use a kill to hide, cover up, distort, lie about their identity. And, in fact, night kills are the least reliable sort of evidence, because wolves can do them without providing any public rationale for their decisions. So, yes, I want to draw the focus away from idle speculation regarding why the wolves killed Modi.

Lastly, Iron's role is most likely survivor. I don't know whether his role is at odds with human or wolf victory, but it is a risk that his would take priority. I recommend against allowing him to live if he does not, fundamentally, play the game as a human. Here are my reasons for thinking this is his role:
(1) His vote on Swoop was opportunistic and poorly supported, regardless of swoop's alignment.
(2) He has stayed staunchly unopinionated throughout the bandwagon on me, which would keep him out of harm's way regardless of which side has wolves involved.
(3) He suggested that a third faction exists:
Quote from: Iron on December 10, 2013, 07:57:33 PM
Or perhaps I smell a third faction
