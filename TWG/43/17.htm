  • Quote

The day gets heated as it draws to a close, even more than the previous day. Opinion shifts in wild swings, a mob-like mentality swinging from player to player until it is split between Trientalis and Swoop.

"Swoop!" cries Trientalis, "How could you betray me? You're my deputy! Guys, don't you recognize me?? I'm the Sheriff for crying out loud!"

The rest of the town is surprised by this outburst. Swoop scratches his head and says, "Uhh, I'm not really sure what you're talking about, Trient. I like, don't even know you. Besides, we all know who the sheriff is, right guys? It's uhhh... Wait, who is the Sheriff?"

A ripple of murmurs ripples across the crowd. "Is she the Sheriff?", "I thought we didn't have a sheriff...", "Isn't Death Butt the Sheriff?", "She could be the Sheriff, I don't really remember." Nobody can quite remember who the sheriff is, or indeed, if the town of Mystic River actually had one. "Surely there was someone who was acting as Sheriff, wasn't there?" "Come to think of it, the Sheriff was the one who found the body in the first place. Should we even trust the Sheriff?"

Swoop Doggy Doge calls out to the crowd, "Let's lynch this liar! Look at how she's connived and tricked us, and is now pretending to be a figure of authority in order to have her way! Surely, Trientalis is the murderer!" The crowd roars enthusiastically as Trientalis is dragged to the gallows.

"Wait, wait!" she screams, "You're making a mistake! Swoop is the real murderer! Him and Narwhal and guy are all in on it together! Mark my words!" But her pleas fall on deaf ears.

Someone wearing a Sheriff's hat and star appears on the gallows and addresses the crowd, "I am the Sheriff, and I always have been! Kill this imposter!" The townsfolk stare at the self-proclaimed Sheriff and look puzzled. Is this the Sheriff who's been leading the town the past few days? It could be, who knows? It's strangely hard to remember. It would be good if someone had a picture of the Sheriff or something so they could check, but none can be produced. The town collectively decides to go with it. Who are they to doubt the Sheriff?

"If you're the sheriff," shouts eep, "Then how come that Sheriff is wearing the hat and star?" The crowd murmurs appreciatively at this question.

"That Sheriff stole them from me!" cries Trientalis, "Please bel..."

Her words are cut short as the block is knocked from under her feet. Trient jerks her body violently as her face swells and turns a bright red. The town watches silently as the struggle for precious life fades from her body until she is swinging slowly, gently, in a good approximation of a simple harmonic oscillator.

The clocktower chimes its mournful tone six times as the sunlight fades behind the trees.

"Fuck," says Iron, "Somebody needs to fix that clock."


Trientalis is dead. Night 2 begins.
