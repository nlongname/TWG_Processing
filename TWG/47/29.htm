Anyway, I suppose I might explain my thinking behind this idea about bandwagons. We're in a big game. A big game with only a few, weak roles (I don't imagine the mystery role will be any more powerful than guardian or seer) and limited information gathering potential. The wolf seer especially hampers that, as they will potentially be able to claim dead people's roles.

This all leads me to have little faith in anyone's suspicions during the first few days, including my own. Our chances of individually or collectively identifying a wolf's suspicious behavior are only slightly better than random selection. Therefore we want to try and force more overt wolfy behavior. We also want to increase our chances of wolves being in bandwagons. But we also don't want to spread our votes too thin, as this lets the wolves gain control and/or hide.

So, a single bandwagon is pretty useless. Poor chance at success, little information to be gained. Three bandwagons is potentially okay at this point, since that means four players each. Two bandwagons is close to what I'd consider ideal, and it's best if they're fairly even. As for 'giving' the lynch to the wolves, that's really not a big deal at this point. We have three humans for every wolf. I'd gladly trade two humans for one wolf, or one human for decent information.

The thrust of all this, though, is that yesterday's scattered voting was pretty lame and I want to avoid making that mistake again. I want people to realize that their votes are more effectively used in bandwagons and if nobody is agreeing with their choice, they should switch to a more popular option or put in the effort to make a serious case.
