While certain roles claiming now could certainly be useful, it's also quite a bit dangerous. The wolves have had two nights with a role seer and, while their chances of having killed a role that they can claim aren't super great, they're better than our seer's chance of having seen a wolf. Basically, anyone could be a wolf right now (except maybe guy) and if we get a claim that's not countered from a wolf, there's not going to be any way to know that for a while unless the seer also claims, which puts the seer at a lot of risk and also starts placing guy at risk. The wolves are unlikely to claim if they know that the real person still exists.

Later in the game, however, we'll have much more information about who we think is wolfy. True, any claim made later is more likely to be a wolf claim than a claim made now, but with the extra knowledge brought about by time, I think we might be able to do more with later claims than with current claims.

As I see it, a current claim will either be a wolf, in which case we only have hope if the seers alive, and even then we might lose our seer to NK and get no more information that way. There's other possibilities, but none of them seem to be great for us. A later claim is more likely to be wolfy, so we can go ahead and be super suspicious of later claims plus we'll still gain the benefits of the knowledge that those roles have through whatever manipulation they choose to partake in. And we can even use that knowledge to verify later claims made.

Still, I think it's a pretty close call whether to claim early or later. But as I see it, with three wolves, a claim made now isn't going to get rid of more than one wolf. Assuming all three are still alive, that's not a huge sacrifice on their part.

i'm looking at it from a slightly different angle (angel)


currently, there are 12 living players

if we assume we've killed 0 wolves and that i'm human, it means there are 11 unknowns, 3 of which are wolves


for every player, this number is 10 because they know themselves. for masons, it's 9, assuming their partner still lives



so, if we also assume that the seers views are both alive and that i was not a view (this is strictly ideal and with the growing number of assumptions this scenario obviously becomes less likely), coming out now would give us the identity of 3 more players (seer, view, view)

even if they're all human, this would leave most vanilla players with 7 choices left for who is wolf. (if they are one of the views, this number is 8. if a view is dead, this number is also 8 ). if we're assuming 3 wolves are left, vanillas would have 7-8 choices of who the remaining 3 could be instead of 10

i posit that this is a great benefit and is wholly better than waiting.

the additional benefit of this is that the seer coming out doesn't give wolves additional information outside of who the seer is. the only people they'd have left to look for with the wolf seer power is the mason pair and the additional role slot. they'd still have to check the people the seer did because the seer can't differentiate between a vanilla and an aux role, while the wolf seer can. so it would be giving the humans more information than the wolves

in the event of a counterclaim, i think we'd actually get more information, just not right away. the wolves have to guess whether i'd guard or not. and even if i didn't and the claimant dies, it confirms everything they've said and nets us a wolf. if they don't, they have to deal with the seer being around confirming more things


if we're looking for 3 wolves in 7, we only have to pick 4 players we believe to be human


we also have to remember that as time goes on, the number of identities the wolves know increases much faster than this.

currently(again, assumptions), they know 4 of 12 alive currently (3 wolves, 1 me), so there are 8 unknowns. if we assume their views have not been lynched (slightly more likely than seer views persisting because their views won't be night killed unless they found xazarus to be seer or mason), they know 2 more things, bringing their unknowns to 6. of these 6, they are looking for 3 players specifically (1 seer, 2 masons). this is also assuming they didn't find one of those with the n2 view

day4, they'll have killed one of those unknowns night3, so it's possible that they'll only have 4 unknown things


my main point is that while on it's face it seems seer coming out is a bad idea and provides the wolves with too much information or something, i think it actually provides humans with much more and doesn't give the wolves that much and takes away some of the power they have. we even have the potential to completely nullify the wolf seer if both masons and seer come out. the only power the wolf seer has is in finding things out before we do. and they only have that power if we let them
