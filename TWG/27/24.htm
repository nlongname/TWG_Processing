The ITEMS – I wanted lots more subtle roles than a game this size allows, so I put a lot of those powers into items.
(click to show/hide)
1. Synthesuit Mail – This symbiote suit can let a villager mimic a hero’s item use. For example, a villager using this mail in conjunction with the Bow of Fire and Rain would be able to use the Bow for kills.

You put on the Synthesuit Mail. You feel a slight shiver as it burrows into your flesh, connecting to vital areas and reinforcing them.
You gain no new powers, but you do gain a new friend: An ancient mystic personality is embedded within the Mail, and it chats happily (but vaguely) with you about the state of the world, about the heroes on the Tablet (apparently he is the preincarnation of one of those heroes; he won't say which), and how impressive digital watches are.
The personality refuses to answer your questions and in fact does not seem to have a very good awareness of what has passed.

2. Pipe of Ages – This pipe allows anyone to channel Boris Worm, giving them the ability to break ties in item votes.
You snatch the Pipe of Ages from the Vault. As you test out its sound, you feel a tingling in your ear. You hear the legendary voice of Boris Worm, Dragon Expert and Bard, Gleeman extraordinaire! He thrills you with a flow of rapid buzzwords - sounds like the kind of guy who'd talk anyone's ear off.
Boris gifts you with just a taste of his power to lead and persuade - from now on, ties relating to itemvotes will be settled by you. Whoever you voted for, if among the tied characters, will automatically receive the item (so the GM will no longer be making a coinflip). At any time, you may shoot the GM a private message about whom you urge the crowd to select for item ownership.
-Your vote breaks itemvote ties in favor of someone who's received your vote
-You can tell Iron ahead of time who should win in the case of ties, if you like.

3. Anvil of Truth – This allows Dr. Agon or Haplo special privilege. It confirms Haplo as human by granting him the Sword of the True Hero or allows Dr. Agon to see a role – but Dr. Agon must reveal his view.
The Anvil feels light and warm under your arm, but doesn't change you noticeably. You test it out, make a few simple throwing daggers, drop it on a few mice and an unlucky roadrunner, but don't see anything magical other than the convenient weight.
You also feel the oddest urge to stand on it, posing boldly in the sight of everyone, but the villagers all just assume that's something heroes do

4. The Crown of Obsidian – Replaces the Mark. Grants the wolves an extra teammate only if placed on a civilian.
You place the Crown on your head. You immediately scream silently within yourself. Someone is trying to get]into your head, to rearrange things, to alter vital situations in your life that have defined who you are. Your heroic will proves too much for the intruding presence, however - the memories of the battles you've fought and the people you've saved in righteousness are too positive for the lurking entity wriggling its way into your brain's crevices. Your fantastic willpower chops at those tendrils, severing them.

Nothing has changed. You are still Elytra, the Blind Seer, enemy of the Dragonfriends. You've gained some inner strength from this encounter, though, and people will probably see you as a stronger and more ruthless person now.
Shuriken of the Constellations – enables Eep to be a Gemini Twin (Mason) with Haplo or enables any wolf to be a false mason.
Bow of Fire and Rain - was coming up soon, haha. You guys didn't need it.
Miracle Herb - a much later item for if the game was taking a long time



The HEROES -
(click to show/hide)
1. Trientalis: You are Elytra, the Blind Seer. At the beginning of every night, you should send a PM to me with the name of the one player you'd like to view. As night ends, you will be told that player's alignment (human or dragonfriend).
See the Iron Tablet in the thread for more information on your character, if you like. And PM me with any questions.

2. That_Kid: You are Valeria, the Rogue Mistress.
Your character is not detailed on the Iron Tablet.
Valeria, the Rogue Mistress – Valeria has thrown off the strictures of society. Dressed in dark greens and blues (black doesn’t blend in as well as you might think) she stalks the night and day alike as it pleases her. Typically, she is armed only with her wrought titanium lockpick collection and her long hardened fingernails. Any more weapon than this would be unnecessary.
This role replaces Vigilante. ONE time during the course of the entire game, you may send me a PM with the name of the player you would like to kill. That player will be killed during the night.
If you can get your hands on the Bow of Fire and Rain, you will be granted an extra kill.

3. Guy: You are a Civilian. That's sort of boring, but perhaps an Item of Power might give you some power of your own...

4. Eep: You are a Civilian. That's sort of boring, but you know that the Shuriken of the Constellations will grant you a small link to the Heroes of the realm. There might be some other Item of Power to help you gain power of your own...

5. Enig.matic: You are a vanilla Dragonfriend (Wolf). Your co-dragonfriends are AJ and Dr. Agon. You may speak with the other dragonfriends at will.

6. Dieter: You are Davíd du Lac, the Miracle Worker (Guardian). Once per night, you can resurrect one player (with your stash of Miracle Herb) if they are nightkilled. You must send me a PM with the name of the player you wish to protect in this way. You may protect yourself.
See the Iron Tablet in the thread for more information on your character, if you like. And PM me with any questions.

7. AJ: You are a Dragonfriend (Wolf), the Captain Dragonrider. Your co-dragonfriends are Dr. Agon and Enig.matic. You may speak with the other dragonfriends at will.
As long as you are alive, the Dragonfriends will get a bonus kill on Night 3 and Night 6.

8. Dr. Agon: You are a Dragonfriend (Wolf). Your co-dragonfriends are AJ and Enig.matic. You may speak with the other dragonfriends at will.
SPECIAL: If you get your hands on the Anvil of Truth, you can use it to gain knowledge of the ROLE of one player. However, the Anvil will force you to reveal this information in the public thread, using a phrase given to you at that time.

9. Haplo: You are Rob Johnson, the True Hero. You must find a way (perhaps an Item?) to proclaim yourself the True Hero - Iron will support you in this if you should gain the power.
See the Iron Tablet in the thread for more information on your character, if you like. And PM me with any questions.

10. Ben: You are a Civilian. That's sort of boring, but perhaps an Item of Power might give you some power of your own...

The recipient will not be a player, and you will not be able to identify the Item as it will appear to be entirely mundane until a tied lynch battle. You know from experience that your Demonseeker will simply babble in Abyssal if you ask him more about who the role might be.

This Item will only count for tied lynches and nothing else whatsoever.
It is the Miracle Worker the Dragonfriends seek.

I was pretty happy with activity levels, for the most part.

Dr. Agon is right in that the Items contribute to inconvenient day/night cycle qualities. There's probably some way to set them up more carefully, but he is the only one impacted by it lol.

N0: Both sides start out with (1d3-1) items in the hands of NPCs. By luck, this is 2 per side.

D1: Item given to Trienalis, who can't use it. I put this item early so that civilians would have a chance to have minor/fat chance powers early on, since personally I think civ is boring. It will prove crucial in a tie later on, though.
Lynch: tied by eep between Enig.Matic and Ben. This tie is broken by Trientalis' item. Throughout the whole game, no one even imagines that Enig might have been a wolf.

N1: Wolves kill Haplo, I'm not sure why, though it's not a bad decision as a confirmed human would have been a big deal in the endgame. Dieter guards himself.

Trientalis views eep, who she's been sparring with. This really worked out well in her favor I think, it seems people used the turnaround as evidence for her.

D2: The only coinflip of the game, which ironically is for an item which removes coinflips entirely. Eep gets the item, which does exactly what it says.
Lynch Ben for inactivity.

N2: Wolves go for Dieter, who is guarding himself. Every night he asks me if it's legal for him to guard himself. Most of the guardians in previous games can (though not all) and his PM explicitly says he can, so I laugh at this because his self-guard was successful twice (vs vig and vs wolves). A bit of confusion in the day over why there was no kill; Dieter takes his time about revealing it was him and not the bulletproof (although he wouldn't know he was targeted).

Trientalis views AJ, who is a wolf.

D3: Dieter given item, though he doesn't want it, I assume people are using their instinctive strategy to vote for less active players.

Quests handed out to humans and wolves.
Dieter notes that this shows two wolves are alive. This is invalid, as quests were set up ahead of time and unknown numbers of NPC items were to be expected, with only the role the wolves need to kill left unrolled, but this is still a good point and something to keep an eye out for. I'll look at future possibilities more critically. I suspect a few of these subtle things got skipped in reading the massive original post, since Dr. Agon also suggests using ties to filter wolves during lynch.

Lynch AJ on Tri's say-so. Dr. Agon and AJ kinda throw their lots together, while I thought AJ's arguments sounded weak. Then again, I knew he was a wolf so I guess it is just awesome to be God. The wolves were really relying on AJ, because he grants them an extra kill tonight if he lives.

N3: For some reason I absolutely cannot understand, Dr. Agon kills eep and confirms Tri, which allows her to out him next day. I'm guessing reverse psychology? Hard to say if this had any effect or not.

That_kid is trying to decide on a kill. He trains his bow on AJ during the day.Then Dr. Agon, then Guy. Now back to Dieter. He's on a forum.

Finally goes with Dieter, which wastes his kill. I don't send any sort of confirming messages because I wanted it to be unknown explicitly what happened during the night, but I guess I should have sent SOMETHING, because I have to answer questions about whether he still has a kill left.

D4: Item not given much consideration. This Item could have really changed things, as it's a Mark. I don't think the wolves know about it, though, which kind of weakens their position.

I don't really understand Dieter's argument against voting for Dr. Agon, but again I know he's a wolf so I'm probably just am play gods. Not enough votes leave Dr. Agon, though, and the game is won.




Humans won by luck and because the dragons collectively were some of the least available players. Too bad; I would've liked to check the balance.
I gave a value to each role (based on some googling and some fiat based on my game) and kept a running pivot table; humans were ahead for a long time since no one power role can equal even the weakest wolf and a wolf was killed first day.
