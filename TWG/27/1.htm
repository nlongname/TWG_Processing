TL/DR VERSION
(click to show/hide)
Quote
Let me know privately if you don't get a PM, or if you have questions. Check out the index of previous games.


The usual rules apply: no quoting PMs, don't talk during the night unless you are a wolf, don't post after you're dead (Famous Last Words are acceptable I suppose), don't talk to each other outside of posting in the thread, etc.

Some potential roles are listed by character name in the thread - claiming a name is nigh identical to claiming a role. This list is not exact.


In addition to the typical lynch votes, there will also be a vote for who gets the item displayed in the showcase each day.
These items contribute to tied lynch votes, and *may* have other special powers.
Items cannot be traded.
The items are handed out before the lynch, in the late afternoon gametime.
Please vote for who gets an item in italics, and who gets lynched in bold.
No vote limits. You can even vote for yourself. There is no instant lynch or itemgrab.


Dragons = wolves, villagers or heroes or humans = humans
Wolf win condition: Destroy the world, be more numerous than humans.
Human win condition: Paint the altar with the blood of all the wolves




Timeline:

GAME DAY:
1% - Reveal nightkill
1% - Reveal item description
1% - Thoughts on when the day will end given (lots of activity may extend the day; once a final sunset time is given it will be followed)
70% - Item voting ends, item given
100% Lynch voting ends, item given

               2500 years ago, a group of bloodthirsty warriors was busy conquering their entire continent. They did it with an extraordinary loss of life - legends say they were powerful mystics.


Mystics whose knowledge of many things was supernaturally exceptional: the knowledge of offensive and defensive magics, the knowledge of war, and the knowledge of the future.

They were eventually wiped out by a terrible unforeseen disease.

               However, before they died, they foresaw a terrible, worldwide cataclysm which would rend the Earth to its core. To assist in the prevention of this disaster, the culture put together a collection of artifacts and scattered it throughout the world. They prophesied that a (very brief) Age of Legends would arrive, where heroes would gather these artifacts and bring them together, and they left behind a calendar to mark the date.
 
           Well, the calendars they left behind say the date is drawing near, and the expected heroes have been hard at work gathering together the necessary artifacts while the rest of the world thinks the date is a whole year in the future.

          It was revealed that a number of villains, the Dragonfriends, were expected to oppose this plan in their attempt to destroy the world. The Dragonfriends are shapeshifters who can take a wolf or human or dragonoid form. You, the Heroes of the Age of Legends, have stepped up to defeat these Dragonfriends and end the Rending of the World.

You have several artifacts to help you in your quest:

      -Your abilities and fighting strength: Players will decide, as the blood-red sun sets each day, who they would like to kill. Whoever has the most votes is the intended victim. Barring special circumstances, that person will by lynched in the general melee. The High Priest demands a blood sacrifice every day or he fears the Earth will be destroyed.

   If there are still Dragonfriends alive after sunset, they can see in the dark and they're gonna whomp someone's heroic human ass (again barring special circumstances).

If all the Dragonfriends are defeated, the world will NOT end. : )

No vote limit

Please put your lynch votes in bold.

-The Iron Tablet of Heroes and Scum:
This tablet defines the expected players in this game. Attempts to change the writing on it do not work, and so the Wizard is convinced that the information about each player is fairly accurate, though it does not give any information about how exactly to identify these heroes and villains, or even how many of them are supposed to be present (some may not be player characters - they thought they were called to be office workers. Oops.).

Metagame information: You will know the in-game names of some potential roles. Thus, roleplaying or dressing up as a character may be seen as a claim, but there will be none of that nonsense about "maybe guessing that the vanilla bean is a human."


-The Iron Vault of Legends :
At the start of each day, details will become available on an Item of Power.
At hottest part of each day, a cell of the Vault of Legends will open and an Item of Power will become available.
In the hands of a hero, the Item will assist to turn the tides when both alignments are evenly matched.
In the hands of the right hero, the Item may even grant a huge advantage. If the villains get their talons on it, however, it could be devastating.
When an Item is grasped by a player, it forms a powerful bond with that character and cannot leave the player's hands.

Both sides (including nonplayer characters) have opportunities to gain Items outside of the Vault, so knowledge of the location of Items is never actually helpful in determining alignment, since the total number of Items is unknown.
However, getting the items in the hands of humans does increase the chances of lynching a wolf in the event of a tie.
Because of this chaos, it may be in the best interest of the wolves or the players to avoid a tie.

Metagame clarification:
In the event of a tie lynch vote, the number of Items of Power in the hands of the humans is compared to the number in the hands of Dragonfriends.
You have no idea of how many items each alignment has.
If one of the potential lynch candidates is of the alignment that has less Items, that character will be lynched above the other tied characters. If this case does not apply, Quetzlcoatl forbid we must move to a secret coin flip.
Therefore, no information about alignment should be explicitly revealed by the resolution of a tie, but your actions will have an effect on the tie.
If no votes are received, the Vault turns and no one gets the item. This is a valid strategy, but remember that players can vote for themselves.

Please put your vote for the receiver of the Item in italics.

-Your wits:
The game should be winnable regardless of roles. I hope that I have given everyone a chance to contribute.





The Iron Tablet of Heroes and Scum
Scruff Melrose, the Artificer –
(click to show/hide)
Scruff is a master craftsman, the kind who wears magnifying goggles, a labcoat, and a vacant expression, and has long spindly dexterous fingers. He is in charge of constructing items of siege, supply lines, new weapons, buildings, and clockwork gadgets of all kind.

Elytra, the Blind Seer –
(click to show/hide)
Elytra wears bright blue contact lenses in the daytime to hide her milky, unseeing eyes. Her throwing daggers are razor sharp, however, as are her other senses. She has a Second Sight in combination with incredible hearing, and thus can often detect what the creatures of the night are doing.

Valeria - [chipped iron, sharpie scribble]

Davíd du Lac, the Miracle Worker –
(click to show/hide)
A newcomer to the area, Davíd is fleeing from the undue attention he received from Dragonfriends in his home country. He travels in a nondescript grey cloak, but when the hood is thrown back his blond hair and sharp face bespeak of lordliness and nobility. The superstitious natives who knew Davíd spit and call him a necromancer, but the men he saved with a caress of his hand and the High Priest all speak of him as a saint.

Boris Worm, the Gleeman –
(click to show/hide)
Boris is a Minstrel and Dragon Expert of unknown origin. You can detect him from a mile away by virtue of his music, jokes, and cloak of many brightly-colored patches sewn together. A charismatic, persuasive man, he is often the leader of any group he joins, but he also deals a nasty cut in battle with his frosted longsword.

Marcus Meat, the Boltproof –
(click to show/hide)
Marcus is known for being nearly unstoppable in battle, due to his racial ability (from a long-lost tribe) to enter a bloodrage. He often fights off troops of men at once with his gigantic two-handed sword. Often wears only the merest of armor to demonstrate his ferocity.

Rob Johnson, the True Hero –
(click to show/hide)
Deadly with bow or with a sword in each hand. Often imitated, but never matched. Personable, eloquent, handsome, and brave, Rob Johnson is the Chosen of all Ages; whenever history tries to repeat itself, he can be found in the front lines fighting for what is right time and time again. The Dark Wolves of Ages Past hate the sound of his name, and if conditions are right the Dragonfriends and the Dark Wolves have sworn to end him.

T[coffeestain], the [inkblot] –
(click to show/hide)
Dressed all in black, pale of face, and lightning-quick, this hero is quick to put down evil, even if it means sacrificing the comfort of himself…or of others. He carries a massive, entropically-charged battle axe which vamps the power of others and wields thaumatic powers with alacrity. His back is turned on society, but he’s committed to the World, and that’s what counts.

Betzyl Saimon, the Arbiter –
(click to show/hide)
the representative of the ancient calendar-writers, Betzyl is a native South American of direct descent from the mystic warriors. He still dresses as they do, with holy serpent necklace on bare chest and feathered headdress, and shares many of their ancient powers. He might know a little something extra about the workings of the Vault or the Tablet, he says…

Castor and Pollux, the Gemini Twins –
(click to show/hide)
Separated at birth in the sweet heady air of early summer, these twins found each other as the Age of Legends dawned, since the pain and thoughts of one are often transmitted to the other. One still goes dressed in the rags of the streets and underworlds where he has lived all this time, and the other is dressed in a three piece suit of the finest cut and holds great financial power. Their magics when they are together make the Dragonfriends spit and curse in anticipation of the Cataclysm.

Villagers –
(click to show/hide)
From every walk of life, from all cultures between the High Wastelands and the Taurine Archipelago, all across America and across the sea and to far-reaching unknown continents, humans of merit and foresight and strength are banding together to support the Heroes. They can look like practically anything, barbarian or businessman, but they oppose the End of the World with everything they’ve got.

Here, the tablets are cracked…perhaps there are other heroes.
