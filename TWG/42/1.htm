You are all out to eat at Milliway’s, the Restaurant at the End of the Universe.

Yes, you are watching the heat death of the universe from a well-shielded window on the planet Magrathea, several quintillion years in the future. Groovy decorations are everywhere and the atmosphere is fantastic. An empty hamster cage sits near your table, with an exercise wheel. The exercise wheel has a hoopiness level of 11.5.

You are all researchers for the Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy, a distinctive guidebook on how to travel across the galaxy on the cheap. Your queries at the U.S. Treasury as to the conversion between galactic currencies and U.S. dollars were not returned for some reason.






That’s about when Arthur Dent keeled over, mostly dead, from some poisonous cheese on his Arcturan Mega-Taco. Marvin, your Android, is out on some business and so could not save him. Who could have committed this crime?

Unfortunately, the GM (GuideMaster?) Iron lost his plot sheet in a cross-country move, so you don’t have many clues.

“From what I can piece together in my investigations,” says Ford, “it all has something to do with a rather boring country called Syria. Apparently they’re all fighting over something. A game of cricket, maybe?”

“No, someone was testing chemical weapons on lab animals. Guinea pigs? Probably both human and nonhuman guinea pigs, in fact,” said Trillian.

“Oh yes. And America is shut down for maintenance, right, and can’t be the world police at the moment. Sweeping up all the crumbs that were attracting vermin or something.”

“No, I don’t think that’s right,” said Zaphod’s first head, adjusting the fish in his ear. “I’m pretty sure it had something to do with this Miley Cyrus cat, the second worst poet in the Galaxy, yeah?” Zaphod’s second head agrees, after a brief consultation.

something something Eddie, the Shipboard Computer

You all put your head(s) together and decide on a course of action. Zaphod is keen on killing everyone in the room one by one until the murders stop. Trillian is completely opposed: “Arthur was a peaceful guy!” Suddenly, Zaphod’s Joo Janta 200 Super-Chromatic Peril Sensitive Sunglasses go completely dark, meaning something dangerous is about to happen.

“Well that settles it, doesn’t it?” say Zaphod’s head. “What could be more dangerous than daily lynchings?”

So it is agreed.
