Guide Entries:
Mankind



Let's skip ahead to Day 6, which is a good bit and has got Marvin in it.

By the way, I've finally got my plot notes in hand, and just in time too! No worries!

Zaphod is trying to explain something to Trillian. Trillian, an ape-descendant, is perfectly capable of understanding thanks to her degree in astrophysics. Zaphod, however, is a politician and therefore has no idea what he is trying to explain.

"So picture a cosmic bathtub, right? Like, a nice porcelain one with gold feet and the latest in laser drain technology, yeah?"

"Are you trying to explain the origin of the universe to me again, Zaphod?"

"No, no, babe, this is something different. Check it out: In the bathtub, you could be at, like, the drain, maybe. Or you could be at the spigot, yeah? It's all the same, dig?"

"Not really, but go on."

"So it's like a difference in space but not in time! But it could be the other way around, right? It could be a difference in time but not in space, right? Well the Restaurant we're in is like that! It's built on Magrathea (remember when we went to Magrathea?). Died off completely about a million zillion years ago. Some sort of...weasel, I think...got in the life support system and gummed it all up."

"What does that have to do with the wolves, Zaphod?"

"I'm getting to that! We've been getting closer all along, you know. And we better hurry, too, 'cause I don't think we have much time. We're all about to be dead guys if we don't act fast!"




Just then, the door between the Restaurant and the parking lot bursts open. In walks a plastic robot with a crude wooden leg and lined with rusty bolts.

"Marvin! You're back! We're saved!" shouts Trillian, running to him.
"Hey, if it isn't my favorite cool cat, Marvin!" says Zaphod.

"If I had a sense of humor...which I haven't..." droned Marvin, "I would say you are all about to wish I was a cat. Is that amusing? I hear cats are very droll."

"Why would that be amusing, Marvin?" asks Trillian.

"Oh. You haven't heard. Then I'm afraid I have some bad news," replies Marvin in an emotionless voice.

Outside the protective dome, the heat death of the universe is winding down with a spectacular display. The great chrono-winches reel the Restaurant back across time to prepare for the next sitting.

Just then, the walls begin to crumble like cheese filled with too many holes. The controlled atmosphere begins leaking out, and poisonous vapors begin leaking in. Enormous four-legged, low-bodied robots tunnel their way in, putting the Krikkit warbots to shame.

The white mice in their cage escape just as a ninth appears from thin air, and all run toward the robots. Hatches pop open at the approach of two of the mice, who climb inside and take control to smash at the remaining rodents. Laser cannons pop out to point at AngryNarwhal.

WITH THESE MAGRATHEAN TECHNOLOGIES WE WILL RULE THE UNIVERSES proclaim the mice from their loudspeakers.

YOU. ANDROID. YOU WILL READ TO US THE ANSWER TO THE ULTIMATE QUESTION OF LIFE, THE UNIVERSE, AND EVERYTHING. IT IS PRINTED ON THE EARTH FEMALE'S BRAINWAVES. READ IT BEFORE WE DESTROY HER.

"I can read it," says Marvin. "But you're not going to like it."

READ IT!

"It says..."
"Database Error."


Wolves have achieved parity with humans. Wolves win!
