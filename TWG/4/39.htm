Notes:

If a role was based on some existing role, it is denoted.

RED
1. Medic (guardian) - Phil
-can übercharge one target per night
-makes this person invulnerable during the night

notes: Pretty bold coming out as "spy." Ultimately proved his undoing, but I cannot blame him for it - he couldn't forsee everyone coming out and also yelling at him.

2. Soldier - Randomizer
-on guard duty in the intelligence room
-if the intelligence is killed, it is his duty to report it to all

notes: Thought his role was useless. Didn't have much to say, so nor do I.

3. Pyro (seer) - Azrael
-spy checker determines allegiance, but not role

notes: Really, why the hell did you come out D1? Could've easily survived a bit longer.

4. Scout - Reaper
-25% chance to dodge*
-this effect is always active and never used up*

*-altered to single-use, 100% success rate

notes: Got pretty lucky that I decided to change his role at the last minute. Not really sure why he got night killed. Relatively low-key otherwise.

5. Heavy - Mystic
-"Get behind me, doctor!"
-one night, he may choose to redirect the medic's über to himself, assuming that he is alive
-the medic will be informed of what happened, but not who did it
-if the heavy is already targeted, he will be informed of this
-if the medic is dead, the heavy will be informed

notes: MVP (tie). Not exactly an exceptional performance, but he did expose Haplo, even if he himself doubted it.

6. Sniper (vigilante) - That_Kid
-headshot: 75% chance to kill the target
-single-use, only usable during the day

notes: Killed Ben for absolutely no reason. Such a waste.

7. Engineer 1 (masons) - Ben
-engineers work best in a team - if the other engineer dies, you learn his identity

notes: I mean, if it makes you feel any better, the medic survived to get killed later that night.

8. Engineer 2 (masons) - Lymric
-engineers work best in a team - if the other engineer dies, you learn his identity

notes: Didn't understand the (potential) usefulness of his role. Guess I shouldn't have watered it down so much. Pretty inactive.

9. Spy - Enigmatic
-must claim to be a different role each round
-if someone goes inactive, you assume his role

notes: Nobody really doubted his 'seer' claim, since AJ had come out. I really wish he hadn't gotten killed right away; would've been pretty amusing to watch everyone's reactions.

10. The intelligence (baker) - LiquidAngel
-if you die, the humans lose in two days
-you may wish to keep a lower profile than normal

notes: You blew it for the RED team. Your role was designed as a liability, and that's exactly what you were: a liability. Somehow, you guys still won.

BLU

11. Demoman (terrorist) - Haplo
-if he is lynched, he kills the first person to vote for him

notes: An okay performance. Didn't really convince me all that much, but it worked in-game.

12. Spy - Guy
-may choose one night to cloak, during which he will appear to be human if viewed

notes: MVP (tie). You almost won it. I was hoping you would attempt to confirm yourself as human, but I guess I can see why you decided against it (didn't know there was only an alignment seer). Pretty convincing human, but I think you were too conservative, as compared to usual. On the whole though, good job.

-RED team wins



So anyway. The idea of the game was that everyone should have a role: there are no civilians or villagers in TF2. Of course, to make this much easier to balance, I had to make most of them weaker, passive, conditional, or flat out a weakness. Hence pyro (seer) lost ability to learn role + alignment, engineers (masons) did not know each other's identities at the start, some chance elements were introduced, etc.

I have more to comment on the game, but I'm pretty tired of typing at the moment. More to come later.
