It was fun in a party form the one time I played it, but this still has potential I think. It's a pretty different world online vs off for these games.

I think it's reasonable to suppose it's likely that one of the mission team members was a spy, assuming eep is telling the truth about her voting (and nobody is counterclaiming so...). I just can't imagine the spies voting unanimously for an all-resistance group and it seems reasonable that said spy would choose to support it to remain hidden. Two spies is unlikely because of the unanimous votes (spies would want one) and the quick resolution.

Therefore I think we should pick one person from the previous team and pick the remaining three from the rest of us. Picking two of the three would be risky (1/3 chance of not picking a spy, assuming one of them is). Picking four from the other seven is also out of the picture; if there really were no spies in the last mission then you'd guarantee picking at least one spy.  If one of the three was a spy, you'd still need to pick the only four non-spies in the category. Picking one from last mission and three from the rest of us gives us the best shot, provided one finds it plausible that a member of the previous mission was a spy.

Thinking aloud though, I wonder if it might actually be a good strategy to try to pick two or more spies for this next round. That way we would force them into a sort of prisoner's dilemma that could possibly end up either saving us another mission or revealing 2/4 players to be spies. Minimizing our chances of picking a spy probably maximizes our chances of picking exactly one spy, who could then sabotage the mission as one hidden among four players.
