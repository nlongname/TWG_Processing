Which would you rather know: That there's at least one spy among three people, or that there's at least one spy among five people?

I don't think that knowing either of these is particularly useful. That's why I want to wait until people have posted more before I approve any team.

For instance, if there is only 1 spy within a group of 3 people, then it is actually better for us to choose at least one of those three for the next team; the odds of any individual member of that team being a spy (1:3) are less than the odds of any member of the rest of the group being a spy (3:7). But that's the entire problem; we don't know that there's only one spy on that team, and if there are more, then we actually want to select from the group that hasn't gone on a mission. And if we have no data on most members of the team, then we're basically just in the same position tomorrow, only with 1 less mission available to win. If there is a spy on this team, they have no reason not to sabotage the mission. We have not gained enough information today to tease out who they are, or how many members of the team are spies.
