I thought it was weird how you guys were trying to put lots of spies on one team to try and catch them in a conundrum. Maybe that strategy could work, but I've always had the best success being methodical about it. I.e. carefully pick the first team, which will 95% of the time succeed even if there are spies on it. Then slowly add people to that same team in later missions. Sometimes you get lucky and get mostly or all Resistance on the team. Sometimes you get unlucky and it can be hard to figure out who the spy on the original team was. But it works way better than just randomly picking an entirely new team for each mission.

Also, in the full game, there are special cards that can be assigned by the first leader of each round. So these can do things like assuming leadership from another player, showing your allegiance to one other player, voting first and publicly, seeing how someone voted on a mission, etc. I didn't include them this time since I thought it might be a little too confusing. But they are a good way to add other dimensions to the game.
