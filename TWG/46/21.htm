Quote from: eep on March 19, 2014, 04:57:23 PM

His role is very much more powerful than regular seer, as he is claiming it. basically, all he needs to do is go through the list comparing pairs. Odds are, any wolves or third party individuals would stand out as being on opposite teams. There's no need to compare directly with himself; in fact, there would be more data if he didn't.

I'ma do the math.

There are fifteen players other than Phil. Assume Phil is human. Assume 4 of them are wolves. Assume no third parties.
Assume he compares two at random the first day, and can use info from previous days to snowball his efforts.  To do less math, I'll usually assume the proportion of wolf:humans is the same, and thus Phil's base chances are the same, until he has a damn good chance of catching a wolf.

We will test eep's assertion that comparing to himself is less valuable.

D1:
6% chance he picks two wolves = "Same team."  (4/15 chance of first pick being a wolf, AND 3/14 chance of second pick being a wolf, order doesn't matter, 4/15*3/14).

52% chance he picks two humans. "Same team." (11/15 chance of first pick being a human, 10/14 chance of second pick being a human, order doesn't matter, 11/15*10/14)

42% chance he picks one wolf and one human. "Different team." (the remainder).

If I had this role and used this strategy, I would probably assume that a Same Team meant two humans (it almost always does), and a Different Team meant one wolf and one human (it does, minus third parties). I think this is what eep was getting at?

He has vaguely equal chances of getting told Same Team or Different Team.
If he gets Different Team D1, he has moved two players from 25% wolf (the base rate) to 50% wolf.
If he gets Same Team D1, he has moved two players from 25% wolf to 10% wolf (6 out of 58 times, Same Team indicates wolf).
Valuable, but better than a seer? not yet.

Number of viewed people: 2 possible.


D2:
Phil now has either a nearly-confirmed human, or a 50% wolf.
The former is more useful for further data. Phil's choices are to compare among a 50% wolf, 10% wolf, or 25% wolf (Different Team, same team, and unviewed, respectively).

If he chooses to compare a new couple, he can create more of those 50% wolves and 10% wolves.

If he compares a D1 Different Team to an unknown:
No matter what Guy says, Phil is unsure whether it's because of two wolves or two humans or what, but it's more likely that his new guy is a human according to base rates...
So getting Different Team increases the chance that his D1 pick was a wolf, and gives him a D2 pick who is more likely human.
Getting Same Team inverts those chances.
This isn't a great strategy but it is better than nothing.

If he compares a D1 Same Team to an unknown: He is taking a nearly-confirmed human. This is similar to using himself, except 10% worse.
If he hears "Different Team", he will probably assume that his D2 new pick is a wolf! This is exciting.
If he hears "Same Team", he can assume both his targets are near-confirmed humans.
Using either of these choices in the future will decrease his chances of being right by a bit.
This is a great strategy, but he only has a 58% chance of being able to use it D2. It creates a pair whose alignments are pretty sure.

Number of people viewed: As many as 4 possible, but 3 viewed with the great strategy.


D3:
Phil can now combine any of those numbers above, depending on what he chose the day before and what he heard.

If he combines two near-confirmed humans:
Hearing "Different Teams" means he was wrong.
Hearing "Same Teams" means he was right.
Uh, great? It's D3 and you've confirmed two people's alignments and only if they're human. That's worse than seer so far.

If he combines two previously-compared 50%, one from each Day:
"Different Teams!" could be a D1 wolf and a D2 human, or the other way around, with equal probability.
"Same Teams!" Same of which team? Both are equally likely.
He doesn't learn shit. Much worse than seer.

If he combines a near-confirmed human with an unknown:
Same power as D2.

If he combines a whole new couple:
Same power as D2.

Number viewed: Up to 6, with about half probably being near-confirmed but no one 100% confirmed. He can sacrifice number viewed to increase his surety. This is an awesome thing and guy did a great job if Phil is not lying.

A seer will never live past D3, but you can see how it would go.

Discussion:
Phil is not objectively a better scumhunter than a normal seer overall, except that he has flexibility.
If he is lucky, he can (from his viewpoint) confirm two humans after 3 days of work.
He has a good chance of knowing 3 people's alignment with decent confidence after 2 days, which is somewhat better than seer but has a chance of being wrong, and won't always happen.
Phil can increase his confidence by reducing his viewed count, or vice versa. He can compare to himself to become a normal seer.
Phil is probably less useful than a seer D1, and probably a little more useful D3.
Even if I did the math wrong, I am probably close enough that what I've said is true.

Thoughts?
