I'm trying to think of how that impacts our strategy.

First of all, if Phil is human, it means he should use the strategy that makes him work like a normal seer: comparing himself to others.

Our greatest strength is probably that we can use genders, night deaths, and patterns to try to guess the wolves. However, this relies on two major assumptions:
-Genders are related to the dueling packs (Seems to be an assumption multiple people are confident in)
-Wolves have an advantage in eliminating the other pack. They'd need a win condition that requires the other pack dead or they'd need a reason to want complete control of the NK. Or however it works.

What would a dueling pack win condition look like? They win if the other pack and all humans are dead?
I know I win when all wolves are dead. It would be strange if humans need to kill all wolves and wolves need to kill wolves and humans. Guess the marking accomplished that? It just doesn't make much sense to me in this or the jugglebug game.

If wolves require the other pack dead, that's an overlap between the goals of the two alignments. Anything we can do to capitalize on it is good. Killing off one pack first isn't necessarily an advantage, but it could be easier to gain that kind of knowledge - and it wouldn't be a step backward, to my mind, because I don't really care who has control of the nightkill. To me, that implies that if you have a choice between two known male wolves and one known female wolf (her partner unknown), take out  the males first. None of that changes the fact that if you see a wolf you should kill it.

I am interested to see what objections Narwhal has to the idea of killing only one gender. Is it because it's too broad (which I agree with) or something to do with the actual wolf reaction?
